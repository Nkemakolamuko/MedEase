Paracetamol side effects

Paracetamol (acetaminophen) is a pain reliever and a fever reducer. The exact mechanism of action of it is not known. Paracetamol is used to treat many conditions such as headache, muscle aches, arthritis, backache, toothache, colds, and fevers. It relieves pain in mild arthritis but has no effect no effect on the underlying inflammation and swelling of the joint.

Safety Tips

Stop using this medication and call your doctor at once if you have a serious side effect such as:

  1. 1. Low fever with nausea, stomach pain and loss of appetite,
  2. 2. Dark urine, clay-colored stools; or
  3. 3. Jaundice (yellowing of the skin or eyes).
